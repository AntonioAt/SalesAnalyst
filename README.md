# BIG SALES DATA DATA PREPARATION REPOSITORY

PROJECT SUMMARY

The analysis covers 10 retail outlets, 1559 product SKUs, and 14204 transaction records. The objective involves cleaning raw data and generating insights regarding product performance and outlet efficiency.

​METHODOLOGY

SQL Data Cleaning: Standardized fat content labels, imputed missing weight values using per-SKU median with a category-level median fallback, imputed missing visibility values, and categorized 16 item types into 6 main categories.
Dashboard Visualization: Created KPI dashboards to segment revenue by category, outlet type, district, and fat content.

​KEY FINDINGS

1. Chain Supermarkets dominate with a 64.8 percent revenue share across 6 stores.
2. Premium Supermarkets hold the highest transaction average at $3819.
3. Regional Districts contribute 42.6 percent of total revenue.
Frozen and Snack products lead revenue with $7.54 million.

​STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

Conduct a feasibility study and operational cost analysis before expanding the Premium Supermarket. Relying on a single outlet sample to justify expansion ignores economic variables.
Prioritize expansion in the Regional District.
Expand the Reduced Fat product line to align with consumer preferences.
